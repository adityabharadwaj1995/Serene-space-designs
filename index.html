§ 02 Philosophy

We do not decorate. We design the air between objects — the pause between rooms, the weight of a doorway, the hour a wall meets the sun.

A practice founded in Milano in 2009 by Elena Caradonna and Tommaso Reni. Our work is residential, slow, and devotional to the climate of a place.

§ 03 Practice
01

Architecture

Residences, villas and retreats — drawn from the orientation of a site, the season of its light, and the memory of its stone.

02

Interiors

Tactile rooms composed in stone, oak and linen. Each interior is a slow inventory of materials chosen for the way they will age.

03

Material

A continuous study of stone, plaster, timber and metal. Carried out with quarries, mills and artisans across Italy and Switzerland.

§ 05 Process

Four acts.
One long conversation.

  1. I.

    Listening

    We begin with silence. A first visit to the site, in the season you intend to live there. We take photographs of the light, not the walls.

  2. II.

    Drafting

    Six months of pencil. Plans, sections, models in plaster and cardboard. We share drawings — never renders — until the architecture is settled.

  3. III.

    Materialising

    Stone is chosen at the quarry. Oak is selected from the log. Every threshold, handle and joint is detailed by hand.

  4. IV.

    Living

    We return after a year. We adjust a curtain, a shelf, a door. A house is finished only when it is forgotten.
